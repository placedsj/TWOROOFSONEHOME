## 2024-05-24 - Accessibility for Modal Close Buttons
**Learning:** Found that the "Agreement Lexicon" and "Parenting Directive" modals use icon-only close buttons (a bare `<X size={...} />` component inside a `<button>`). While visually intuitive due to standard UX patterns, these buttons lack accessible names and announce only as "button" or "unlabeled button" to screen reader users, causing navigation confusion in critical legal/agreement contexts.
**Action:** Always inspect custom modal implementations and floating action buttons for missing `aria-label` attributes. When a button's content is solely an SVG or an icon component, explicitly provide an `aria-label` describing its function (e.g., "Close lexicon" or "Close reader") to ensure parity between visual and programmatic accessibility.
